Embark on an aquatic adventure in "Bishop Isles," a captivating video game where you take on the role of a courageous fisherman defending his tranquil town from an invasion of bizarre sea creatures. The innovative Wii-like controller, equipped with gyroscopic capabilities, adds an extra layer of excitement to the game. Players can physically swing the controller to unleash attacks, creating an immersive and engaging combat experience. With a seamless blend of technical expertise, teamwork, and creative flair, "Bishop Isles" promises an unforgettable journey through underwater realms, combining classic gaming nostalgia with cutting-edge design.

Vehicle Black Box
Project Description

In my notable capstone project, which secured an impressive third place overall, our collaborative team embarked on a mission to revolutionize vehicle safety and emergency response efficiency. This ambitious endeavor involved the meticulous development of a sophisticated system designed to connect seamlessly with a myriad of sensors, ultimately providing users with real-time insights into the condition of their vehicles. Our holistic approach aimed not only to enhance safety but also to significantly reduce the response time of first responders in critical situations. The intricacy of this project was underscored by the successful integration of cutting-edge components, including but not limited to a vibration sensor, temperature sensor, gyroscope sensor, GSM module, LCD, and Arduino Mega 2560. Each component played a pivotal role in the creation of a high-performance system, where the amalgamation of diverse sensors ensured comprehensive vehicle health monitoring. Leveraging our expertise in the C programming language and Arduino IDE, we orchestrated a seamless implementation, optimizing the functionalities of each element for optimal performance. The success of this capstone venture not only attests to our technical proficiency but also underscores our commitment to innovation and the application of advanced technology in addressing real-world challenges.

The Pathway Project - Vision Galveston Hackathon

Accomplished First Place Winner at the Vision Galveston Hackathon in October 2023 by leading a highly skilled and motivated four-person team. Our team conceptualized, developed, and implemented the groundbreaking "Pathway Project," a comprehensive initiative designed to assist homeless individuals in Galveston by revolutionizing navigation and access to essential resources.

The "Pathway Project" not only addressed the immediate needs of the homeless population but also aimed to create a sustainable impact. I played a key role in pioneering the design of a cost-effective and innovative "resource kiosk." This kiosk served as a centralized hub for information and support services, featuring an interactive map for navigation assistance and access to vital resources.

What set our project apart was the incorporation of a solar-powered system to ensure environmental sustainability and autonomy. The kiosk also featured an advertising display, strategically integrated to generate revenue for maintenance during periods of inactivity. This dual-purpose design demonstrated our commitment to both social impact and innovative problem-solving through the fusion of technology and strategic thinking.

Our success in the hackathon underscored the effectiveness of the "Pathway Project" in addressing the challenges faced by the homeless population, showcasing the potential for technology to drive positive social change.
